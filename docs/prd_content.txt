1. Executive Summary
South Africa's stokvels represent one of the most significant untapped opportunities in retail banking. Over 11 million South Africans participate in stokvels, collectively contributing an estimated R50 billion annually — yet virtually all of this flows through cash, WhatsApp groups, and informal trust networks, entirely outside the formal banking system.
Digital Stokvel Banking is a bank-native feature set that brings this deeply rooted cultural savings practice into the formal financial ecosystem. By meeting customers where they already are — in their savings groups — the bank gains access to a massive, underserved segment while offering genuine value: interest on pooled deposits, digital transparency, fraud protection, and a pathway to formal credit.
This PRD defines the requirements for an MVP launching within 3 months, targeting Android, iOS, USSD (feature phones), and web browser platforms. The primary revenue model is interest margin on pooled deposits.
Strategic Opportunity at a Glance

  Largest informal savings vehicle in the country — untouched by major banks
  Average stokvel group: 10–20 members, R500–R2,000/month per member
2. Problem Statement
2.1 The Stokvel Participant
Despite being a disciplined saver, the average stokvel member faces several structural disadvantages by operating outside the formal system:
No interest earned — pooled funds sit idle in cash or a basic account, eroding in real value due to inflation
Fraud and theft risk — large cash payouts at year-end are a known target for crime
No credit record — consistent monthly contributions are invisible to credit bureaus, offering no benefit to the member's financial profile
Administrative burden — managing contributions, tracking who has paid, and resolving disputes is entirely manual
No financial safety net — missed contributions cause social conflict with no formal resolution mechanism
2.2 The Bank
From the bank's perspective, stokvels represent a relationship gap:
Existing stokvel members are often already bank customers, but their stokvel activity generates zero value for the bank
The informal nature of stokvels means R50B+ in annual flows bypass the bank entirely
No existing product adequately serves the group savings use case with cultural sensitivity and appropriate features

5. Feature Requirements
5.1 Feature Priority Framework
Features are classified into three tiers:
P0 — Must have for MVP launch (3-month target)
P1 — Target for Phase 2 (3–6 months post-MVP)
P2 — Longer-term roadmap (6–12 months)
5.2 Core Feature Set
F-01  Group Creation & Management  [P0]
Any bank customer can create a stokvel group and invite members.
Create a named group with a description, group type (rotating payout, savings pot, investment club), and contribution rules
Invite members via phone number, share link, or QR code
Assign roles: Chairperson, Treasurer, Secretary
Set contribution amounts, frequency (weekly/monthly), and payout schedule
Members must have or open a bank account to join (onboarding flow embedded)
F-02  Digital Group Wallet  [P0]
All member contributions flow into a single, bank-held group savings account.
Pooled funds held in a dedicated group savings account earning tiered interest
Real-time balance visible to all members (role-based view)
Full contribution history — who paid, when, how much
Chairperson can view, but not unilaterally withdraw without quorum approval (governance rules)
F-03  Contribution Collection  [P0]
Members contribute digitally, eliminating cash risk.
One-tap payment from linked bank account
Debit order/recurring payment option for automatic contributions
USSD dial-in payment for feature phone users (e.g. *120*STOKVEL#)
Payment reminders 3 days and 1 day before due date (push, SMS, USSD)
Contribution confirmation receipt issued to member and logged on group ledger
F-04  Payout Engine  [P0]
Automated, rules-driven payouts replace manual cash distribution.
Rotating payout: automated disbursement to designated member each cycle
Year-end pot: full balance disbursed to all members proportionally at year-end
Chairperson initiates payout; Treasurer must confirm; system executes
Instant EFT to member bank accounts
Payout notifications to all group members for transparency
F-05  Group Governance & Dispute Resolution  [P0]
Formal governance structures that mirror how stokvels traditionally self-govern.
Group constitution builder — define rules for missed payments, late fees, member removal
In-app voting for major decisions (e.g. change contribution amount, remove member)
Missed payment escalation: automated notice, grace period, and chair notification
Dispute flag — member can raise a dispute; bank mediates if unresolved
F-06  Multilingual Interface  [P0]
Language is a trust signal. The app must speak to users in their language.
Full UI available in: English, isiZulu, Sesotho, Xhosa, Afrikaans
USSD menus also available in all 5 languages
Language selection at onboarding, changeable in settings
F-07  Credit Profile Builder  [P1]
Stokvel contributions become proof of financial reliability.
Opt-in: member can consent to contribution history being shared with credit bureau
Consistent on-time payments generate a positive 'Stokvel Score' indicator
Score visible in banking app and can be referenced for loan applications
Bank pre-qualification offers triggered based on Stokvel Score thresholds
F-08  Financial Wellness Nudges  [P1]
Contextual, non-intrusive financial education embedded in the product flow.
Post-payout: 'Your R3,200 payout earned R48 in interest this year — here's how to grow it more'
Contribution streak milestones (e.g. '12 months of perfect payments — your credit profile is building!')
Annual stokvel summary report: contributions made, interest earned, payout received
F-09  Stokvel-to-Investment Bridge  [P2]
For groups that accumulate significant savings, a path to collective investment.
Group can vote to move a portion of savings into a bank-offered unit trust or fixed deposit
Investment held in group name; all members see performance
Unlocks a new revenue stream for the bank (investment management fees)
F-10  Insurance Integration  [P2]
Group funeral and burial society cover, a deeply important use case in SA.
Group can opt into a bank-facilitated burial society product
Monthly premium deducted from group wallet; payout to family of deceased member
Embedded in the group creation flow as an optional add-on

7. Monetisation Model
7.1 Primary: Interest Margin on Pooled Deposits
All stokvel group funds are held in a dedicated group savings account. The bank earns a margin between the rate paid to the group and the rate at which it deploys those funds.

Daily compounding, monthly capitalisation to group wallet

Partial withdrawal requires quorum approval; full payout at cycle end

8. Cultural & Trust Design Principles
This section is non-negotiable. Stokvels are built on trust, community, and cultural practice. The product must earn its place in that tradition — not disrupt it.
Core Design Principles
  1.  The bank is the infrastructure, not the leader — the Chairperson retains authority within the group
  2.  Transparency is non-negotiable — every member can see the full ledger at all times
  3.  Cultural framing — use 'stokvel', 'umuntu', 'ilungu' in-app, not clinical banking terms
  4.  Governance mirrors tradition — quorum requirements, role titles, and dispute process match real stokvels
  5.  No data is sold or shared without explicit, informed consent — this must be communicated clearly
  6.  The bank never contacts group members directly about products without Chairperson consent
8.1 Language & Tone
Use warm, communal language. Avoid: 'account', 'product', 'KYC', 'compliance'
Preferred: 'your group', 'your savings pot', 'your contribution', 'your community'
Error messages must be encouraging, not alarming (e.g. 'Your payment didn't go through this time — let's try again' not 'Transaction Failed')
8.2 Trust Signals
Bank logo and FSCA regulation badge always visible on group wallet screen
'Your money is protected' disclosure shown on first deposit and on the wallet home screen
Contribution receipts are branded, shareable, and can be presented in group meetings as proof
Group ledger can be exported as a PDF for annual stokvel AGM records
9. Data, Privacy & Compliance
Requirement
Detail
POPIA Compliance
All personal data handling must comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA). Explicit consent required for credit bureau reporting and marketing contact.
FICA / KYC
All group members must be verified bank customers. Non-customers complete simplified onboarding (ID + selfie) before joining. USSD users complete FICA via branch or ATM.
AML Monitoring
Group accounts subject to standard AML transaction monitoring. Thresholds: flag groups with single deposits >R20,000 or total monthly inflows >R100,000.
Credit Bureau Reporting
Opt-in only. Member must provide written (digital) consent. Clear explanation of what is reported and how it affects their credit profile.
Data Residency
All data stored on South Africa-domiciled infrastructure (SARB requirement for banking data).
Group Data Ownership
Contribution history, member roster, and group ledger are owned by the group. Bank is a custodian. Members can export their data at any time.

11. Risks & Mitigations
Risk
Impact
Mitigation
Cultural resistance — members don't trust a bank to hold group funds
HIGH
Chairperson-first design. Bank is never 'in charge'. Group rules are enforced by the group, not the bank. Run community roadshows with trusted community figures.
USSD session reliability on low-cost networks
HIGH
Partner with all major MNOs (Vodacom, MTN, Cell C, Telkom). Session state persistence. Fallback SMS confirmation for all transactions.
Regulatory classification of group savings account
MEDIUM
Engage SARB and FSCA early. Position as a bank-held savings account in group name — not a collective investment scheme.
Fraud — internal group disputes and false contribution claims
MEDIUM
Immutable digital ledger. All contributions are bank-verified. Dispute resolution process with bank escalation path.
Low smartphone adoption in target segment
MEDIUM
USSD is a first-class platform, not an afterthought. Feature parity for core flows. USSD-first marketing in low-income communities.
Competition from fintech (TymeBank, Stokvela, etc.)
LOW
Bank advantage: trust, FSCA regulation, interest-bearing accounts, credit integration, existing customer relationships.

13. Appendix
A. Glossary
Term
Definition
Stokvel
An informal rotating savings club or credit union. Members contribute regularly and receive a lump sum payout on a rotating basis.
Umseki / Chairperson
The leader of a stokvel group, responsible for administration, collecting contributions, and managing payouts.
Ilungu / Member
A participant in a stokvel group.
USSD
Unstructured Supplementary Service Data — a protocol for real-time mobile communication used for banking on feature phones via short codes (e.g. *120*321#).
POPIA
Protection of Personal Information Act — South Africa's primary data privacy legislation.
FICA
Financial Intelligence Centre Act — South Africa's KYC/AML regulatory framework.
FSCA
Financial Sector Conduct Authority — the conduct regulator for financial institutions in South Africa.
NIM
Net Interest Margin — the difference between interest earned on assets and interest paid on liabilities, expressed as a percentage.
Stokvel Score
A proprietary indicator (Phase 2) derived from a member's stokvel contribution history, used to supplement traditional credit scoring.
